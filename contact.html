About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Biomedical Engineering at UC Irvine, working in the Gratton Lab. My research focuses on integrating advanced fluorescence imaging, such as phasor-based hyperspectral microscopy, with machine learning to study subcellular mechanisms of chemoresistance in triple-negative breast cancer.

Throughout my graduate work, I’ve developed tools for high-dimensional image analysis, unmixing spectral signals, and tracking subcellular dynamics. I’m passionate about bridging physical principles with biological discovery and aim to contribute to translational research that impacts patient care.
